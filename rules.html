Football XG Game

Rules

  • When you click 'start game' the 90 second countdown will begin.
  • You have four options: long pass, short pass, dribble or shoot.
  • The computer will randomly attempt to block a long pass, short pass or dribble. If they are successful you return to the half way line.
  • If the computer is not successful, you advance closer to the goal.
  • A long pass will advance you further, but the computer is more likely to try and block this.
  • Don't be predictable: the computer will learn. If you repeatedly press the same button the computer will defend against it.
  • When you shoot, there is a percentage chance it will go in. The closer you are to the goal the better the percentage...

What is XG?

XG stands for "Expected Goals" and is a statistic used in football to measure the quality of a chance. It takes into account factors like shot location to calculate the likelihood of scoring. Since the introduction of expected goals, teams have concluded that, in general, continuing to fashion a chance very close to the goal has a higher probability of scoring than a long range shot from distance.
